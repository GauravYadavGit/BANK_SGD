In our project, we used a shared EMR cluster rather than provisioning a cluster per pipeline. The cluster was sized to handle multiple concurrent workloads, with one master node, around 6 core nodes, and auto-scaled task nodes depending on demand.
Our pipeline was one of several running on this cluster, so we configured our Spark job to use around 6 to 8 executors with 2 cores each, consuming roughly 12 to 16 cores during execution. Resource allocation was managed by YARN, which allowed multiple pipelines to run simultaneously without fully occupying the cluster.
We also ensured that critical financial pipelines had higher priority through queue-based resource allocation. During peak loads, we leveraged auto-scaling task nodes to handle additional demand and avoid performance bottlenecks.
This setup allowed us to balance cost efficiency with performance while meeting our SLA of delivering processed data within one hour.
